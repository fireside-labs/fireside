# Pricing Strategy — Agent Marketplace

---

## The Product

Valhalla agents are not software. They're **trained workers.** The pricing should reflect training time and specialization, not lines of code.

| Pricing Factor | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Days evolved | More time = more procedural memory = more instinct |
| Crucible survival rate | Higher = more reliable knowledge |
| Procedure count | More procedures = wider capability |
| Domain specificity | Niche expertise commands premium |
| Cloud model costs incurred | Seller's training costs baked in |

---

## Tiers

### Free Tier — Community Agents

**Price:** $0

**What's included:**
- Open-source agents shared under MIT license
- Community-trained, community-maintained
- Typically <30 days evolved, <100 procedures
- No crucible testing guarantee
- "Community" badge on marketplace listing

**Examples:**
- General coding assistant (starter agent)
- Basic research helper
- Simple automation agent

**Purpose:** Onboarding funnel. Get users into the ecosystem. Show them what an evolved agent feels like. Then they want better ones.

---

### Paid Agents — Individual Sale

**Price range:** $5 – $99

| Price Point | What Justifies It |
|---|---|
| **$5 – $15** | 30-60 days evolved, general purpose, 100-500 procedures |
| **$15 – $35** | 60-120 days evolved, domain-specific, 500-1500 procedures, 90%+ crucible |
| **$35 – $99** | 120+ days evolved, deep specialization, 1500+ procedures, 95%+ crucible |

**Revenue split:** 70% creator / 30% platform

**Why 70/30:**
- Apple/Google take 30% and provide distribution + trust
- We provide distribution + trust + crucible verification
- 70% keeps creators motivated to train and publish quality agents
- Industry standard for digital marketplaces

---

### Premium Collection — Subscription

**Price:** $29/month or $249/year

**What's included:**
- Access to all "Valhalla Verified" agents (curated, 95%+ crucible)
- New agents added monthly
- Early access to agents from the core team
- Priority support

**Revenue allocation:**
- 60% to creator pool (distributed by installs)
- 40% platform

**Why subscription:**
- Predictable revenue for the platform
- Creators get ongoing income, not one-time spikes
- Users don't need to evaluate every purchase — "Verified" badge does the work

---

### Enterprise — Custom Agents

**Price:** Custom ($500 – $5,000+)

**What's included:**
- Agent trained on customer's specific domain/data
- 180+ days of training included
- Dedicated crucible testing
- Custom personality tuning
- Ongoing maintenance (monthly retraining)
- Air-gapped deployment support

**Revenue:** 100% to creator (platform takes 0% — enterprise deals bring users to the platform)

## Trust Signals

The marketplace's long-term value depends on buyer trust. Here's how pricing relates to trust:

| Signal | Where Shown | Effect on Price |
|---|---|---|
| Crucible survival rate | Agent card + detail page | >95% commands premium |
| Verified Purchase reviews | Detail page | More reviews = higher conversion |
| "Valhalla Verified" badge | Card + listing | Top-tier agents in subscription |
| Days evolved | Card stats | >90 days = significant |
| Creator reputation | Creator profile | Multiple successful agents = premium |

## Launch Strategy

### Phase 1 — Seed (Month 1-2)

- **Free only.** Build the ecosystem with open-source agents.
- Core team publishes 5-10 starter agents.
- Focus on install flow, not revenue.

### Phase 2 — Paid Agents (Month 3-4)

- Enable paid listings at $5-$35 range.
- First 10 paid agents get "Featured" placement.
- Creator onboarding guide: how to train, test, and publish.

### Phase 3 — Subscription (Month 6+)

- Launch "Valhalla Verified" subscription tier.
- Curate top 20 agents across categories.
- Annual plan discount (30% off).

### Phase 4 — Enterprise (Month 9+)

- Custom training engagements.
- Air-gapped deployment.
- SLA-backed support.

## Pricing Anti-Patterns to Avoid

1. **Don't let sellers change price after purchase.** Buyers who paid $29.99 don't want to see it at $9.99 next week. Version bumps can have new prices; existing versions stay fixed.
2. **Don't make free tier so good that paid feels unnecessary.** Free agents should be useful but clearly less capable than paid.
3. **Don't hide the crucible rate.** It's the core trust metric. If a $50 agent has 60% crucible survival, that's a problem — flag it.
4. **Don't allow "0-day evolved" paid agents.** Minimum 30 days evolution for paid listings.
5. **Don't take >30%.** Creator incentive alignment is everything. If creators don't earn, they don't publish.
